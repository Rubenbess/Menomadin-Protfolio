**Rylo (fka Nagish)** | Growth | $85M
[MANDATE FIT: HealthTech]

- Investors: General Catalyst and Canaan (co-leads), Cardumen, Vertex Precursor, K5

[MANDATE FIT: Cyber]

- Suggested angle: At $29M Series A this is above Menomadin's typical check size, but the preventive cloud security space is still forming. Identify seed-stage companies building adjacent enforcement or governance tooling for multi-cloud environments.

## Section 3: Market Insights

- **Cybersecurity continues to dominate.** Three of the six confirmed deals this week are pure-play cyber (A Security, Aryon Security, Willow), and a fourth (PointFive) was founded by cyber veterans pivoting into AI infrastructure. Capital invested in Israeli cybersecurity more than doubled versus H1 2025.

- **AI agent infrastructure is a distinct emerging category.** Willow (agent identity/governance) and Upriver (autonomous data pipelines) both target the operational layer that makes AI agents functional inside enterprises. This pattern, building the picks-and-shovels for the agent era, is accelerating across the Israeli ecosystem.

- **Repeat founders attract outsized capital.** PointFive's founders (IntSights, $350M exit to Rapid7) and A Security's angel roster (Wiz, Cyera, Dazz CEOs) illustrate the pattern highlighted in the IVC-LeumiTech H1 report: the share of rounds going to serial founders rose from 34% to 39% in 2026.

- **Hetz Ventures appeared in two deals this week** (Upriver and Willow), continuing its active deployment from the $140M Fund IV raised in January. TLV Partners and Viola Ventures are also recurrent names across recent rounds.

- **The H1 2026 funding report released June 10 confirmed macro trends:** $8.6B raised in H1 2026 (+45% YoY), but deal count declined ~35%. Capital is concentrating in fewer, larger rounds. M&A reached $10.7B across 84 transactions.
