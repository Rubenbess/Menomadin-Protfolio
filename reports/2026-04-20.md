## Section 1: Top Deals

**Credo Technology / DustPhotonics** | M&A Exit | Up to $1.3B
[MANDATE FIT: AI]
- What they do: DustPhotonics develops silicon photonics technology that integrates optical components onto silicon chips, enabling ultra-high-speed data transfer (400 Gbps to 1.6 Tbps) with low energy consumption for AI data centers.
- Acquirer: Credo Technology Group (US). Deal structured as $750M cash, $123M in Credo shares, plus an earnout of up to ~$430M in additional shares.
- Why it matters: Photonics is emerging as a critical bottleneck in AI infrastructure scaling. Jensen Huang identified photonics as a core pillar at GTC earlier this year, and this exit validates the thesis that optical interconnects are the next frontier in data center performance.
- Signals: Chairman Avigdor Willenz is a legendary serial exiter (Habana Labs to Intel, Annapurna Labs to AWS). Investors include Greenfield Partners, Sienna Venture Capital, Exor Ventures, and Atreides Management. DustPhotonics raised over $100M total.
- Suggested angle: Track adjacent Israeli photonics and optical networking startups. The DustPhotonics exit at 10x+ on invested capital will likely catalyze new company formation in this space.

**Artemis** | Seed + Series A | $70M
[MANDATE FIT: Cyber, AI]
- What they do: AI-native cybersecurity platform delivering real-time detection and automated response against AI-powered attacks. Builds a defense layer where AI fights AI in a threat landscape where attacks execute in minutes.
- Investors: Series A led by Felicis, with First Round Capital and Brightmind returning from the $15M seed. Theory VC, Two Sigma, and Lockstep also participated. Angel investors include founders of Demisto and Abnormal AI, plus former CEO and CTO of Splunk.
- Why it matters: This is the largest combined seed-to-Series-A raise in Israeli cyber this year for a company barely six months old. The investor roster reads like a cybersecurity hall of fame, signaling deep conviction that AI-native defense is a generational category.
- Signals: Co-founder Shachar Hirshberg was a development manager at Demisto (acquired by Palo Alto Networks for ~$600M). Israeli founders operating out of NYC with ~30 employees.
- Suggested angle: Artemis is pre-revenue and building fast. The founding team's Demisto pedigree and the speed of capital deployment suggest this will become a competitive reference point in AI security. Worth tracking for follow-on dynamics or co-investment.

**Capsule Security** | Seed | $7M
[MANDATE FIT: Cyber, AI]
- What they do: Runtime trust layer for agentic AI systems. Monitors and controls AI agents as they operate inside enterprise environments, preventing manipulation, data exfiltration, and unauthorized behavior in real time.
- Investors: Led by Lama Partners alongside Forgepoint Capital International.
- Why it matters: As enterprises deploy AI agents via platforms like Microsoft Copilot Studio and Salesforce Agentforce, runtime security becomes a critical gap. Capsule demonstrated this by publishing two vulnerabilities (ShareLeak in Copilot Studio, PipeLeak in Agentforce) alongside its launch.
- Signals: Founded by Naor Paz (F5, Unit 8200) and Lidan Hazout (Securedtouch, Transmit Security). Open-source tool ClawGuard released on launch. Tel Aviv HQ.
- Suggested angle: Agentic AI security is becoming a distinct category, separate from traditional app security. At $7M seed, Capsule is at a stage where Series A engagement in the next 6-12 months is realistic. The founding team has strong exits in their background.

---

**Cisco / Astrix Security** | M&A (In Talks) | $250M-$350M
[MANDATE FIT: Cyber]
- What they do: Astrix provides visibility and security for non-human identities, including AI agents, automated processes, and service accounts, detecting and remediating excessive or malicious access.
- Acquirer: Cisco Systems. Deal reported April 10; negotiations ongoing. No public confirmation from either party.
- Why it matters: If completed, this would be Cisco's largest Israeli cybersecurity acquisition since its push into AI security. Astrix tripled its workforce and grew revenue 5x in the past year, reaching Fortune 500 customers including Workday, NetApp, Priceline, and Figma.
- Signals: Astrix raised $85M total, including a $45M Series B from Menlo Ventures' Anthology Fund (a partnership with Anthropic). Founded by Unit 8200 veterans Alon Jackson and Idan Gour.
- Suggested angle: Non-human identity management is converging with the agentic AI wave. Three Israeli startups in this space (Astrix, Venice, Oasis Security) have raised or are exiting at significant scale in 2026. The category is heating up fast.

**Iron Nation Fund II** | VC Fund Launch | $60M
- What they do: Israeli VC firm investing seed through Series B in Israeli startups, with a focus on medtech, defense tech, and deep tech sectors outside traditional cyber/AI concentration.
- Fund details: $50M of the $60M target already raised. Announced April 13 alongside a $15M commitment from the State of Indiana through the IEDC, establishing a US market access corridor for portfolio companies.
- Why it matters: Iron Nation pivoted from an emergency support vehicle (launched during the 2023 war) to an institutional VC fund. Its portfolio track record already includes illumex, acquired by Nvidia less than two years after Iron Nation's initial investment.
- Signals: Founded by Gil Friedlander, Chen Linchevski, and Jason Wolf. The Indiana partnership provides a structured entry point to the US market with tax incentives and access to Eli Lilly and IU Health ecosystems.
- Suggested angle: Iron Nation's mandate in medtech and defense tech outside the crowded AI/cyber space could surface early-stage opportunities relevant to Menomadin's impact thesis. Worth monitoring portfolio announcements.

## Section 3: Market Insights

- **Cybersecurity dominates the week.** Three of the four startup-level deals (Artemis, Capsule Security, Astrix) sit squarely in cyber, and all three focus on the intersection of AI and security. The theme is no longer "AI for security" but specifically "security for AI agents," a distinction that marks a new sub-category forming in real time.

- **AI infrastructure exits are accelerating.** The DustPhotonics exit at up to $1.3B is the largest Israeli M&A deal announced in April and validates a growing appetite for photonics and physical-layer AI infrastructure. Combined with the Medtronic/CathWorks deal (announced in February, expected to close this month at up to $585M), Israeli deep tech exits are running at significant scale.

- **Non-human identity is becoming a standalone category.** Astrix (potential $350M exit), Oasis Security ($120M Series B in March), Venice ($25M Series A in February), and Linx Security ($50M Series B in March) have collectively raised or are exiting at over $500M in 2026. This level of capital concentration in a single sub-segment of cyber is unusual and suggests institutional investors see NHI as a platform-level opportunity.

- **Israeli VC ecosystem is rebuilding institutional infrastructure.** Iron Nation's fund launch, combined with the $450M Yozma 2.0 program from the Innovation Authority, shows active effort to rebuild domestic VC capacity after the disruptions of 2023-2024. The Indiana partnership is a novel model for US market access that other funds may replicate.

- **Repeat founders and elite military backgrounds remain the strongest signal.** Every major deal this week involved founders with prior exits or Unit 8200 backgrounds. DustPhotonics (Willenz), Artemis (Hirshberg/Demisto), Capsule (Paz/Unit 8200), and Astrix (Jackson and Gour/Unit 8200) all fit this pattern.

- Shachar Hirshberg (Artemis co-founder): Former development manager at Demisto, acquired by Palo Alto Networks for approximately $600M.
