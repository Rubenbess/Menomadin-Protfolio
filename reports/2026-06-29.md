## Section 1: Top Deals

**AppsFlyer** | Series E | $1B+
[MANDATE FIT: B2B SaaS]
- What they do: Mobile attribution and marketing analytics platform measuring ad performance, tracking ROAS, fighting ad fraud, and providing cross-platform measurement for advertisers and publishers. ~$500M ARR, profitable, ~1,300 employees.
- Investors: Google, Meta, Unity, Moloco (each taking minority, non-controlling, non-exclusive stakes)
- Why it matters: Four competing advertising giants jointly investing in the same measurement company is unprecedented. It signals a structural shift toward neutral, independent ad measurement infrastructure as the industry moves to agentic marketing workflows.
- Signals: Strategic round rather than traditional VC. All four investors are platform rivals agreeing to co-own the referee. No investor receives preferential API access or commercial treatment.
- Suggested angle: Too late for Series A entry, but the deal validates the broader ad measurement and marketing analytics stack. Track Israeli startups building adjacent or complementary tools in attribution, privacy-compliant analytics, or AI-driven media optimization.

**Engram** | Seed | $98M
[MANDATE FIT: AI]
- What they do: Builds a "learned memory" architecture that separates reasoning from memory in AI models, claiming up to 100x reduction in token consumption while maintaining model performance.
- Investors: Kleiner Perkins, General Catalyst, Sequoia (co-leads); Andrej Karpathy (OpenAI co-founder), prominent Israeli tech investors
- Why it matters: A $98M seed at a $600M valuation for a 13-person company reflects extreme conviction in AI efficiency infrastructure. As enterprises grapple with runaway token costs, memory-layer optimization is becoming a standalone category.
- Signals: Triple top-tier VC co-lead is rare at seed stage. Karpathy involvement provides deep technical validation. Israeli co-founder Dr. Dan Biderman, formerly of Stanford's AI lab.
- Suggested angle: Token cost and AI efficiency is heating up fast (see also PointFive's $60M Series B earlier this month). Track Israeli founders building infrastructure that reduces AI compute costs for enterprises.

**Traysar** | Seed | $25M
- What they do: Develops autonomous platforms for subterranean military operations, including remotely operated vehicles that navigate, map, and breach tunnel networks, plus rapid-burrowing systems for underground access and payload delivery.
- Investors: Silent Ventures (lead), Lux Capital, Ora Global, Entree Capital
- Why it matters: First company purpose-built for underground defense operations. Post-October 7 demand for tunnel warfare technology has created a new defense category with strong U.S. and allied military interest.
- Signals: $100M+ valuation at seed stage for a one-year-old company. Team includes former Anduril, SpaceX, and Boring Company engineers. Lux Capital's involvement adds Silicon Valley defense-tech credibility.
- Suggested angle: Outside Menomadin's core mandate but emblematic of the Israeli defense-tech boom. Monitor for dual-use technologies that could spin into civilian applications such as mining, infrastructure inspection, or underground utility mapping.

**Lama AI** | Series A | $12M
[MANDATE FIT: Fintech] [MANDATE FIT: AI]
- What they do: AI-native loan origination platform for community and regional banks. Deploys a network of AI agents handling borrower onboarding, document processing, financial spreading, underwriting support, and credit memo generation.
- Investors: EJF Ventures (lead), Fin Capital, 1st & Main, Viola Ventures, Hetz Ventures (existing)
- Why it matters: Community banking is a $1.5T+ lending market still running on manual, paper-heavy processes. Lama AI is one of the first to deploy end-to-end agentic workflows for credit decisioning, turning weeks-long processes into minutes.
- Signals: 300% year-over-year revenue growth. Repeat investors (Viola, Hetz) doubling down. EJF Ventures brings deep banking-sector expertise and distribution.
- Suggested angle: Strong mandate fit at the intersection of Fintech and AI. Round size ($12M) and stage (Series A) align with Menomadin's check range and investment thesis. Founders previously co-founded Levl, acquired by Comcast for $60M in 2022. Worth direct outreach and evaluation this week.

**Limitless Labs** | Series A | $20M
[MANDATE FIT: AI]
- What they do: Develops a physical AI foundation model for precision manufacturing, automating CNC programming by learning the physics of metal cutting, design-file geometry, and machine constraints. Already deployed with Blue Origin, Cadillac F1, Sandvik, and Iscar.
- Investors: Dell Technologies Capital, Square Peg (co-leads), Grove Ventures, Meron Capital, Kinetica
- Why it matters: AI applied to physical-world manufacturing is an emerging category with high barriers to entry. The mechanical manufacturing industry is a $2T+ market still reliant on decades-old CAM software and specialized human programmers.
- Signals: Strategic investor (Dell Technologies Capital) signals enterprise distribution potential. Production deployments with tier-1 industrial clients validate product-market fit. Plans to double Tel Aviv-based R&D team.
- Suggested angle: Represents the "physical AI" thesis gaining investor momentum. The $20M round is slightly above Menomadin's typical check size but the company's Israeli R&D base and industrial focus make it worth tracking for co-investment or follow-on opportunities.

## Section 3: Market Insights

- **AI infrastructure captured the largest share of late-June capital.** Three of the four deals announced in the June 22-29 window are AI-linked: Engram (AI memory), Lama AI (AI agents for banking), and AppsFlyer (AI-powered ad measurement). Across the full month, AI-adjacent companies absorbed over $1.3B, confirming that Israeli AI investment is accelerating rather than plateauing.

- **Cybersecurity deal sizes are expanding to match the agentic era.** NewCore raised $66M at seed stage, Aryon Security raised $25M at Series A, Cyera reached a reported $12B valuation, and the Motorola/D-Fend acquisition closed at $1.5B. The focus is shifting toward AI-era attack surfaces: non-human identity management, agent security, browser-level control, and automated data governance. Capital invested in Israeli cybersecurity more than doubled versus H1 2025.

- **Defense tech is establishing itself as a durable Israeli investment category.** Traysar ($25M seed, tunnel warfare), Shifters ($10.2M seed, battlefield robots), D-Fend/Motorola ($1.5B acquisition, counter-drone), and the broader Kela round ($200M, military OS) demonstrate sustained investor appetite. This sector is pulling talent from elite units (8200, Talpiot, 81) and attracting U.S. defense-aligned funds.

- **H1 2026 totals reveal a concentration paradox.** Israeli startups raised $8.6B in H1 2026, a 45% increase year-over-year. But the number of funding rounds fell approximately 35%, meaning a larger share of capital is flowing into fewer, more established companies. Serial founders now account for 39% of rounds, up from 34% in 2025.

- **Repeat founders are dominating the deal landscape.** PointFive's founders previously built IntSights (Rapid7, $350M). Lama AI's founders co-founded Levl (Comcast, $60M). NewCore's CEO founded Dome9 (Check Point). Cylake was founded by Nir Zuk (Palo Alto Networks). This recycling of entrepreneurial talent and investor networks is compressing time-to-funding and inflating seed valuations.

- **Fintech**: Lama AI's agentic banking platform could displace traditional lending software vendors. Any Menomadin portfolio company in banking infrastructure or credit decisioning should evaluate this competitive dynamic. EJF Ventures' involvement (a specialist banking investor) gives Lama AI distribution into community banks.

- **AI/B2B SaaS**: PointFive (infrastructure cost) and Engram (token cost) are attacking AI cost management from complementary angles. Any portfolio company with significant AI compute or cloud spend is both a potential customer and a competitive benchmark.
