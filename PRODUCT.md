# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Venture capital fund managers, investment partners, and analyst/associate teams. They are financially sophisticated, domain-fluent, and time-pressured. They use this platform during focused work sessions — tracking portfolio health, reviewing pipeline deals, managing relationships, running financial models — not casual browsing. They expect precision, not guidance.

## Product Purpose

Menomadin is a VC portfolio management platform: a centralized command center for managing portfolio companies, deal pipelines, investor relationships, documents, and financial analytics. Success means a fund manager can open it and immediately understand the health of their portfolio without having to dig. The platform replaces scattered spreadsheets and fragmented tools with one coherent, authoritative view.

## Brand Personality

Precise, Trustworthy, Calm.

Think Bloomberg Terminal done tastefully — high information density executed with restraint, not chaos. The platform earns confidence through accuracy and clarity, not through visual excitement. It should feel like something a serious investor is proud to show a founder during a board meeting.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS defaults:** Purple/teal gradients, hero-metric cards with big numbers and gradient accents, identical icon-heading-text card grids, excessive chart decoration. The look every AI-generated dashboard defaults to.
- **Consumer/social apps:** Rounded pastel cards, playful illustrations, overly friendly micro-copy. Too casual for the domain.

## Design Principles

1. **Data earns the space.** Every metric and chart shown must justify its presence. No vanity numbers, no decorative visualizations. Density is only a virtue when the information is load-bearing.
2. **Hierarchy over whitespace theater.** Pack information intelligently using scale, weight, and contrast — not by adding padding until things look "spacious." A VC checking runway shouldn't have to scroll past emptiness.
3. **Confidence through restraint.** Avoid decoration that signals effort. The quality shows in precision: exact spacing, consistent type scale, colors used with purpose. Less is more only when the less is immaculate.
4. **Trust the expert.** Users know their domain. No over-simplified affordances, no hand-holding copy, no excessive confirmation dialogs. Design for someone who uses the platform daily and understands VC terminology.
5. **Consistency as reliability.** An investor checking portfolio health at 11pm shouldn't encounter a layout they have to re-learn. Every screen follows the same structural logic. Predictability is a feature.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as the baseline. Sufficient contrast ratios, full keyboard navigation, screen reader support for tables and forms, and reduced motion respect via `prefers-reduced-motion`. Dark mode fully supported with calibrated color tokens (not just inverted).
