## Section 1: Top Deals

**Airis Labs** | Series B | $60,000,000 (total; $31M in latest round)
[MANDATE FIT: AI]
- What they do: Airis Labs builds an AI platform that connects and analyzes multiple streams of visual information, including security cameras, drones, body cameras, and media footage, and transforms them into structured intelligence that can be searched, investigated, and analyzed in real time. Founded in April 2023 by Noam Friedman (CEO), Amos Lahav, and Rotem Abeles.
- Investors: PSG Equity (lead), TLV Partners, Stepstone Group, Redseed Ventures. Angel investors include Eyal Waldman, Jeff Horing, Yasmin Lukatz, and David Chinn.
- Why it matters: Airis emerged from stealth with $60M already raised, revealing a company that has been building quietly for over three years in the defense and national security intelligence space. The Series B led by PSG Equity, a growth investor, signals that the platform has moved beyond R&D into commercial deployment.
- Signals: All three founders held senior positions across the Prime Minister's Office, Palantir Technologies, and the Israeli defense-tech ecosystem. The combination of deep intelligence community roots and Palantir alumni DNA positions this as a direct competitor to Palantir in specific visual intelligence verticals. The company has 50 employees, mostly in Tel Aviv, with additional staff in Washington, D.C.
- Suggested angle: The visual intelligence and defense analytics category is drawing significant capital. Adjacent Israeli startups building AI-powered analysis for specific data modalities (satellite, geospatial, signals intelligence) are likely fundraising or will be soon. For Menomadin, the takeaway is that dual-use intelligence platforms are valued highly, and earlier-stage companies in this space with commercial applications beyond defense could fit the AI mandate.

**Rep AI** | Follow-on | $6,200,000
[MANDATE FIT: AI]
- What they do: Rep AI develops a unified AI platform for ecommerce that improves customer engagement, conversion, and operational efficiency across the online shopping journey. The platform integrates AI-driven product recommendations, customer service, and sales assistance into a single system. Based in New York and Tel Aviv.
- Investors: Silicon Road Ventures (lead), Osage Venture Partners, Flashpoint Venture Capital, Zendesk.
- Why it matters: Zendesk's participation marks its first investment in an Israeli startup and signals strategic interest in AI-native ecommerce infrastructure. The follow-on funding comes after an $8.2M Series A in August 2024, suggesting steady traction rather than a step-function moment.
- Signals: Zendesk investing as a strategic backer, not just a financial one, indicates potential integration or partnership opportunities. The ecommerce AI space is crowded, but Rep AI's unified approach across the full shopping journey differentiates it from point solutions.
- Suggested angle: The deal itself is small, but Zendesk's entry as an Israeli startup investor is worth noting. For Menomadin, the signal is that large enterprise software platforms are now actively scouting Israeli AI companies for strategic positioning, which improves exit optionality for AI-focused portfolio companies.

## Section 3: Market Insights

- **This was a notably quiet week for Israeli tech funding.** Only two confirmed deals were announced between May 25 and June 1, a sharp contrast to the prior week's six deals totaling over $400M. The lull likely reflects the extended Shavuot holiday period (June 1-2) and a natural pause after a particularly active stretch in early-to-mid May that included Decart's $300M mega-round and Unframe's $50M Series B.

- **Defense-tech and dual-use AI remain the highest-conviction categories.** Airis Labs' emergence from stealth with $60M in accumulated funding continues the pattern of well-capitalized Israeli defense-AI companies revealing themselves after extended stealth periods. This follows Kela ($200M in May), Above Security ($43M in March), and Cylake ($45M earlier this year) in the same pattern.

- **Strategic corporate investors are entering the Israeli ecosystem more aggressively.** Zendesk's first Israeli startup investment (Rep AI) follows a broader 2026 trend of enterprise software companies using strategic investments to access Israeli AI innovation. ServiceNow, Cisco, and Coupa have all made Israeli acquisitions this year. For Series A funds, this expands the buyer universe beyond traditional acquirers.

- **The AI category continues to dominate, with no non-AI deals this week.** Both deals involve AI as a core platform capability. Through May 2026, the Israeli ecosystem has raised $1.71B across 59 equity rounds year-to-date, with AI accounting for the majority of capital deployed.
