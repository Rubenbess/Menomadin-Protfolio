## Section 1: Top Deals

**Decart** | Series B | $300,000,000
[MANDATE FIT: AI]
- What they do: Decart builds chip-agnostic AI infrastructure that accelerates training and inference workloads across hardware vendors. The platform enables AI developers to optimize performance regardless of underlying silicon, and the company is extending into world models and physical AI. Founded in 2023 by Israeli engineers Dean Leitersdorf, Orian Leitersdorf, and Moshe Shalev.
- Investors: Radical Ventures (lead), NVIDIA, Atreides Management, Valor Equity, Adobe Ventures, Toyota Ventures, eBay Ventures. Returning investors Sequoia Capital, Benchmark, and Zeev Ventures. Angels include Andrej Karpathy (OpenAI co-founder), Michael Eisner (former Disney CEO), the Yamauchi family (Nintendo founding family), and Moritz Baier-Lentz.
- Why it matters: At a $4 billion valuation, Decart is now one of Israel's most valuable private AI companies. Amazon joining as a strategic customer alongside NVIDIA as both investor and partner signals that Decart's chip-switching infrastructure has become critical plumbing for the largest AI buyers.
- Signals: The investor roster reads as a who's-who of AI infrastructure capital. Karpathy and Eisner investing personally alongside institutional heavyweights suggests strong conviction from technical and business operators. Revenue is described as "significant" from licensing agreements with cloud providers and AI labs.
- Suggested angle: Decart is well past Menomadin's check-size range, but the chip-agnostic AI infrastructure layer it occupies is generating derivative opportunities. Israeli founders building middleware, optimization tooling, or vertical applications that sit on top of multi-cloud AI infrastructure are worth sourcing.

**Unframe** | Series B | $50,000,000
[MANDATE FIT: AI]
- What they do: Unframe is a managed AI delivery platform that converts enterprise use cases into fully tailored, production-ready AI solutions in days rather than months. The company surpassed $100 million in total contract value within 12 months of operations, with 400% net revenue retention. Founded in 2024 by Shay Levi, Larissa Schneider, and Adi Azarya, all former senior leaders at Noname Security.
- Investors: Highland Europe (lead), Bessemer Venture Partners, Craft Ventures, TLV Partners, Third Point Ventures, Cerca Partners, Vintage Investment Partners.
- Why it matters: $100M TCV and 400% NRR in the first year is an exceptional growth trajectory for an enterprise AI company. Highland Europe leading from the growth equity side at Series B signals confidence in near-term scaling. The company employs roughly 130 people, split between Israel, California, and Berlin.
- Signals: The founding team's exit from Noname Security (acquired by Akamai for $450M) gives them deep enterprise security sales DNA applied to a broader AI deployment problem. Total funding now stands at $100M.
- Suggested angle: Unframe's rapid scaling validates demand for "AI delivery as a service" in the enterprise. Earlier-stage Israeli startups tackling AI deployment bottlenecks, model orchestration, or AI reliability for specific verticals could follow a similar trajectory and are within Menomadin's range.

**Tonkean / Coupa** | M&A | Undisclosed (estimated hundreds of millions)
[MANDATE FIT: AI, B2B SaaS]
- What they do: Tonkean developed an AI-powered agentic intake and orchestration platform for procurement and business operations. The platform automates complex workflows involving procurement, compliance, and supplier management using AI agents. Headquartered in Palo Alto with R&D in Tel Aviv, approximately 80 employees.

The deal signals that agentic AI for procurement and supply chain operations has crossed from experimental to acquisition-worthy. Coupa now claims to be the only platform offering fully integrated Agentic-as-a-Service capabilities for global trade.
- Signals: The acquisition validates the thesis that vertical agentic AI platforms in B2B workflows command premium exits. Israeli R&D teams building autonomous agent capabilities for specific enterprise functions are attracting buyer attention.
- Suggested angle: The agentic procurement category is consolidating quickly. Israeli founders building AI agent platforms for adjacent enterprise functions (finance operations, HR, legal, compliance) are likely next in line for strategic interest. Worth sourcing actively.

**Genie Security / Cyera** | M&A | ~$50,000,000
[MANDATE FIT: Cyber]
- What they do: Genie Security developed AI-driven data loss prevention technology specifically designed to detect and prevent sensitive data leakage through generative AI tools used by employees. The company had a commercial product deployed across hundreds of endpoints in Israel and the United States. Founded by Nadav Noy (Unit 8200 veteran, Israel Defense Prize recipient) and Noam Dotan (IDF Matzov unit veteran).
- Investors: Acquired by Cyera, the data security unicorn valued at $9 billion. Genie had previously raised approximately $3M from Mensch Capital and Dynamic Loop, with Wiz CEO Assaf Rappaport among its angel investors.
- Why it matters: Acquired at roughly $50M just five to six months after founding, this is one of the fastest seed-to-exit cycles in recent Israeli cyber history. Cyera's acquisition spree (now five companies) reflects the urgency to build a comprehensive data security platform as enterprises adopt generative AI at scale.
- Signals: The speed of the exit underscores how acute the AI data leakage problem has become. Rappaport investing as an angel and Cyera acquiring shortly after suggests the Wiz-adjacent network identified this gap early. Cyera is building aggressively through M&A while its $9B valuation gives it acquisition currency.
- Suggested angle: AI data governance and DLP for generative AI is a white-hot category. Israeli cyber founders tackling adjacent problems (AI model security, prompt injection defense, AI compliance frameworks) are building in an environment where rapid acqui-hire exits are realistic. Earlier-stage bets in this space carry favorable risk-reward.

## Section 3: Market Insights

- **AI dominated the week entirely.** Every deal announced between May 18 and May 25 involved AI as a core component, whether in infrastructure (Decart), enterprise delivery (Unframe), agentic workflows (Tribal, Tonkean), analytics (Arito), or data security for AI tools (Genie Security). There was no sector diversification this week.

- **M&A activity concentrated on a single day.** Both the Cyera/Genie Security and Coupa/Tonkean acquisitions were reported on May 21, continuing a pattern of strategic acquirers moving aggressively to absorb Israeli AI capabilities before they scale independently.

- **Enterprise AI agents are the dominant theme.** Three of the six deals (Tribal, Tonkean, Arito) involve AI agents operating autonomously inside enterprise systems. This reflects a market shift from AI copilots (human-in-the-loop) to AI workers (autonomous execution), which is reshaping how VCs evaluate B2B SaaS investments.

- **Cybersecurity exits are getting faster.** Genie Security's acquisition at approximately five months old follows a broader 2026 pattern where Israeli cyber startups are being absorbed before reaching Series A. Cyera, Palo Alto Networks, and Cisco have all acquired Israeli cyber companies within months of founding this year.

- **Repeat founders continue to attract outsized capital.** Tribal's founding team includes operators behind two prior exits (Spot.io to NetApp, Swordfish to Huawei). Arito's founders exited Levl to Comcast. Unframe's team came from the Noname Security acquisition. This week reinforces that the Israeli ecosystem's recycling of experienced founders into new ventures remains its strongest structural advantage.
