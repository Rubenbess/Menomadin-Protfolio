## SECTION 3: MARKET INSIGHTS

- **AI dominance continues.** AI-related deals (AI21, ScaleOps, Sett, Above Security, Notch) accounted for the majority of capital deployed this week. The agentic AI theme is now touching cybersecurity, gaming, finance, and insurance simultaneously.

- **Cybersecurity remains a capital magnet.** Above Security ($43M), Onit ($11M), and Onyx ($40M) all closed rounds in recent weeks. Insider threats and AI agent governance are emerging as distinct subcategories within cyber.

- **Repeat investor patterns.** Lightspeed appeared in both ScaleOps and Notch. Cyberstarts backed both Oasis Security and Onyx Security. NFX led Q-Factor's seed after previously backing early Israeli deep tech deals.

- **Energy infrastructure is attracting serious capital.** Voltify's $30M seed co-led by Aleph and Fortescue is notable. Energy is typically underfunded at seed stage in Israel, and strategic participation from a mining giant signals real commercial intent.

- **Consumer deals are rare but real.** Haat's $20M round is an outlier in an ecosystem tilted heavily toward enterprise. Israel Post as lead investor reflects a broader trend of non-traditional investors entering the startup market.

- **M&A remains active.** The AI21/Nebius talks, if confirmed, would be the largest Israeli AI acquisition of 2026. This follows ServiceNow's Traceloop acquisition ($60-80M) and Medtronic's pending $585M CathWorks deal from earlier this year.
