**Quantum Art** | Series A Extension | $40,000,000 (total round: $140,000,000)
- What they do: Developing multicore trapped-ion quantum computers based on research from the Weizmann Institute of Science. The architecture is designed to scale beyond current qubit limits by connecting multiple processing cores, targeting 1,000+ qubit systems.
- Investors: Bedford Ridge Capital (lead), Hudson Bay Capital, Poalim Equity, LIP Ventures, Wolverine Global Ventures, IDA Ventures.
- Why it matters: The $40M extension (bringing the Series A to $140M total) was driven by continued investor demand, not operational need. This signals that quantum computing is attracting growth-stage capital at the Series A level, a shift from the historically grant-dependent funding model for deep physics companies.
- Signals: The Weizmann pedigree is significant. Israeli quantum startups (Quantum Art, Q-Factor, Quantum Machines) have collectively raised over $300M in the past 18 months, establishing Israel as a global quantum hub alongside the US, UK, and France. Financial institutions (Hudson Bay, Poalim Equity) participating in a quantum round is unusual and suggests commercial timelines are shortening.
- Suggested angle: Quantum computing itself is outside Menomadin's direct mandate, but the ecosystem of quantum-adjacent software, error correction, and quantum-safe security startups in Israel is growing. These adjacent companies may reach Series A within 12-18 months and fit within Menomadin's check-size range.

## Section 3: Market Insights

- **Shekel-dollar crisis is squeezing Israeli startups.** The shekel crossed below NIS 3 per dollar for the first time in over 30 years, representing a roughly 20% dollar decline over the past year. Israeli startups that raise in dollars but pay salaries in shekels are losing 150 to 200 months of combined runway across VC portfolios, according to industry estimates. This is the most significant macro headwind facing the ecosystem and may accelerate fundraising timelines as companies seek to top up eroded reserves.

- **April closed at $1.3 billion in total Israeli startup capital.** Per Globes (published May 3), Israeli startups raised $1.3B across all April rounds. The first four months of 2026 now stand at $4.4B, up 34% versus the same period in 2025. VAST Data's $1B round (covered in last week's report) dominated, but mid-market deals like Aidoc ($150M) and Quantum Art ($140M total) filled out the month.

- **HealthTech momentum is building.** Aidoc's $150M Series E, coming one week after Medtronic's $585M acquisition of CathWorks, creates a two-week stretch of over $700M in Israeli healthtech capital events. Goldman Sachs and Medtronic are not speculative investors. This validates the clinical AI and medical device AI categories at a level that should increase LP interest in early-stage healthtech funds.

- **Quantum computing is clustering in Israel.** Quantum Art ($140M total), Q-Factor ($24M in early April), and the established Quantum Machines collectively represent over $350M in Israeli quantum funding. The Weizmann Institute and Technion are producing a pipeline of quantum-focused founders, and Bedford Ridge Capital's lead in the Quantum Art extension suggests specialized deep-tech investors are forming around this cluster.

- **Deal volume was lower than recent weeks.** The combination of Israel Independence Day (April 22, covered in last week's report), the shekel-dollar disruption, and the transition into May contributed to a quieter announcement period. Expect a pickup as Israel Tech Week Miami (which began April 27) generates follow-on deal activity and new announcements.

## Section 4: Special Flags

**Repeat founders:**
- Aidoc co-founder Elad Walach (CEO) has built the company from founding through five funding rounds totaling over $500M and is now eyeing an IPO. While not a prior exit, the sustained execution across a decade of company-building in a regulated sector (healthcare AI) is notable.
- Fullpath was founded by Aharon Horwitz and originally operated as AutoLeadStar before rebranding. The company's evolution from a narrow lead-gen tool to a full CDP platform, culminating in a ~$500M exit, illustrates the value of vertical SaaS patience.

**Competitive threats to mandate sectors:**
- The shekel-dollar dynamic is a portfolio-wide risk for any fund investing in Israeli companies. A 20% currency swing directly impacts burn rates, hiring costs, and effective valuations for companies with Israeli-based teams. Menomadin should assess currency exposure across portfolio companies and encourage hedging strategies where feasible.
- Goldman Sachs leading Aidoc's round signals that late-stage institutional capital is flowing into Israeli healthtech at scale. This is positive for exit visibility but could compress the window for early-stage funds to build positions in the best healthtech companies before valuations inflate.

**High-priority opportunities worth reaching out on this week:**
- **Definity** is the strongest direct-fit opportunity this week. At $12M Series A with a strategic backer (Dynatrace), the company sits at the intersection of AI infrastructure and B2B SaaS, both Menomadin expansion sectors. The $12M round size is within Menomadin's check-size range for co-investment. The agentic data engineering category is nascent, and early positioning could yield strong returns. Recommend initiating outreach.
- **Quantum-adjacent software startups** emerging from the Weizmann and Technion ecosystems. The Quantum Art extension and Q-Factor seed demonstrate deep investor appetite for Israeli quantum. The software and tooling layer around these hardware companies (error correction, simulation, quantum-safe cryptography) will need Series A capital within 12-18 months. Building relationships with the quantum research groups now could surface early deal flow.
