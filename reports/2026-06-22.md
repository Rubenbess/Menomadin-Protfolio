## Section 1: Top Deals

**NewCore** | Seed | $66M
[MANDATE FIT: Cyber]
- What they do: Identity security platform purpose-built for the agentic era, protecting humans, machines, and AI agents. Rebuilds workforce identity management from the ground up, replacing legacy IAM with a security-first architecture.
- Investors: Pre-seed led by Index Ventures and Cyberstarts. Expanded seed led by Evolution Equity Partners. Angels include Assaf Rappaport (Wiz CEO), Yevgeny Dibrov (Armis), Ofer Ben-Noon (formerly Talon/Argus, now Palo Alto Networks), Ofir Ehrlich (Eon), Sharin Fisher Dibrov (Startpoint Capital), Yotam Segev (Cyera).
- Why it matters: A one-year-old company reaching a $300M valuation on a $66M seed underscores the velocity of capital flowing into identity security for AI agents. This category barely existed 18 months ago and is now attracting the most prominent operator-angels in Israeli cyber.
- Signals: Founded by Zohar Alon (sold Dome9 to Check Point), Amihai Neiderman (founded Nym), and former T-Mobile/Telstra CIO Erez Yarkoni. The angel roster reads like a who's who of Israeli cybersecurity exits.
- Suggested angle: Track NewCore as a potential co-investment signal. Their angel cap table connects to nearly every major Israeli cyber exit of the past five years. Identity security for non-human entities is becoming a platform category.

---

**Limitless Labs** | Series A | $20M
[MANDATE FIT: AI]
- What they do: Physical AI foundation model for precision manufacturing. Given a CAD file, their CAM Agent identifies features, recommends tools, sequences operations, and generates toolpaths, reducing CNC programming time by up to 50%.
- Investors: Led by Dell Technologies Capital and Square Peg, with participation from Grove Ventures, Meron Capital, and Kinetica.
- Why it matters: AI has saturated software and cyber. Limitless Labs represents the next frontier: applying foundation models to physical-world industrial processes where the addressable market is massive and incumbents are decades behind on AI adoption.
- Signals: Dell Technologies Capital leading signals strong enterprise hardware channel interest. Total funding now $27.3M. Expanding a deep-tech research lab in Tel Aviv with plans to double headcount.
- Suggested angle: Series A fund sweet spot. The intersection of AI and industrial manufacturing is under-penetrated by Israeli VCs. Worth monitoring for follow-on dynamics or adjacent portfolio opportunities.

---

**Kimba** | Seed | $6.5M
[MANDATE FIT: HealthTech]
- What they do: AI-powered scent therapy device for improving sleep quality and cognitive recovery. Clinical studies showed 21% improvement in cognitive performance on stimulation nights, with 86% of participants reporting improved sleep.
- Investors: Led by Selva Ventures, with Resolute Ventures, Able Partners, Barrel Ventures, Palette Ventures, Supermoon Capital, Air Ventures, Beyond 22, and Fresh Fund.
- Why it matters: Non-pharma sleep intervention backed by clinical data. The consumer health and wellness market continues to attract early-stage capital, and scent-based therapy is a genuinely differentiated approach in a crowded space.
- Signals: Founded by Ben Fuxbruner, a former IDF special forces commander whose personal experience with PTSD-related insomnia drove the company's mission. Consumer device priced at $299 with recurring scent subscription.
- Suggested angle: Early-stage HealthTech with clinical validation. The $6.5M seed is below Menomadin's check size but worth tracking for a potential Series A in the next 12 months if commercial traction materializes.

---

**Tenet Security** | Seed | $6M
[MANDATE FIT: Cyber]
- What they do: Runtime security layer for enterprise AI agents. Patent-pending "Agent-side Simulation" predicts an AI agent's likely next actions before execution and can intervene to block risky behavior, generating a trace explaining the intervention.
- Investors: Led by The Westly Group and MizMaa Venture. Angels include Tomer Schwartz (Dazz founder, acquired by Wiz) and Lior Tal (former Coralogix CEO).
- Why it matters: As enterprises deploy AI agents at scale, the security gap between deployment speed and governance capacity is widening. Tenet estimates enterprises may be running 5x more AI agents than security teams are aware of.
- Signals: Founded by Sternberg and Nevo Poran, former Cisco cybersecurity researchers who built AI defense capabilities. Previously founded Wild Pointer, which served Fortune 500 clients. Early deployment in the legal sector grew from 2 to 20+ AI agent deployments in six months.
- Suggested angle: AI agent security is becoming a distinct category within cyber. With seed-stage entry, this is a potential Series A candidate within Menomadin's mandate if the founders can convert early deployments into repeatable enterprise sales.

## Section 3: Market Insights

- **Agentic security is consolidating as a category.** Three of this week's four deals (NewCore, Tenet Security, and to some extent Limitless Labs) directly address the security, identity, or automation challenges created by autonomous AI agents. Investor conviction is moving from "AI is interesting" to "AI agents create new attack surfaces that need new infrastructure."

- **Cybersecurity dominance continues.** Two of the week's four deals and the largest round ($66M) are pure-play cyber. This is consistent with the broader H1 2026 trend: cybersecurity investment in Israel more than doubled compared to H1 2025.

- **Repeat investors are concentrating bets.** Brightmind Partners and CrowdStrike CEO George Kurtz appeared in multiple Israeli cyber deals over the past two weeks (Aryon Security on June 11, Pi on June 10). Armis co-founders Yevgeny Dibrov and Nadir Izrael continue to back multiple companies including NewCore. The same small group of operator-angels keeps appearing across deals.

- **Physical AI and deep tech are gaining traction.** Limitless Labs' Series A, backed by Dell Technologies Capital, signals growing institutional appetite for AI applied to physical-world manufacturing. This aligns with the broader Israeli market shift toward deep tech, semiconductors, and hardware noted by the Israel Innovation Authority's Yozma 2.0 program.

- **HealthTech seed activity persists.** Kimba's $6.5M round shows continued investor interest in clinically validated consumer health products. The sector remains active at early stages despite the headline dominance of AI and cyber.

- **June pacing is strong.** The Innovation Authority noted Israeli startups raised over $1.5B in June 2026 overall, continuing the strong trajectory from Q1 where $3.1B was raised (34% up from Q1 2025). H1 2026 total stands at approximately $8.6B.

## Section 4: Special Flags

- **Repeat founders:** NewCore's Zohar Alon previously co-founded Dome9, acquired by Check Point Software. Amihai Neiderman founded Nym. Tenet Security's founders previously built Wild Pointer (Fortune 500 cybersecurity clients) and worked at Cisco on AI defense.

- **Competitive dynamics in AI agent security:** NewCore ($66M for identity), Tenet Security ($6M for runtime security), and recent deals like Capsule Security ($7M in April) are all attacking different layers of the same problem: securing AI agents in the enterprise. This category is fragmenting quickly, which means consolidation is likely within 18-24 months.

- **High-priority tracking:** Tenet Security and Kimba are both at seed stage and could be Series A candidates within the next 12 months. Tenet fits squarely in the Cyber mandate. Kimba fits HealthTech. Both warrant outreach for relationship building ahead of their next rounds.

- **Menomadin portfolio adjacency:** Voltify's $30M seed round (announced March 2026) included participation from the Menomadin fund. Monitor the rail electrification category for follow-on opportunities or adjacent energy-infrastructure deals.
