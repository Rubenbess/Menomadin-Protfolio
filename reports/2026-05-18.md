## Section 1: Top Deals

**LayerX / Akamai** | M&A | $205,000,000
[MANDATE FIT: Cyber]
- What they do: LayerX built a browser-based security platform providing real-time visibility and control over user and AI agent activity across browsers, applications, and IDEs. The platform addresses enterprise AI usage control and secure browsing. Founded in Israel with approximately $45M raised prior to acquisition.
- Investors: Acquired by Akamai Technologies for $205M in cash. Previously backed by investors including Insight Partners.
- Why it matters: This is Akamai's fourth Tel Aviv-based cybersecurity acquisition in five years, reinforcing a deliberate strategy to build an AI-era Zero Trust stack from Israeli components. The deal targets what Akamai calls the most urgent priority for security leaders in 2026: AI usage control inside the enterprise browser.
- Signals: LayerX had approximately $10M in ARR at the time of acquisition, implying a roughly 20x revenue multiple. Browser-based security is consolidating rapidly following CrowdStrike's acquisition of Seraphic and Palo Alto Networks' acquisition of Talon.
- Suggested angle: The LayerX exit confirms browser security as a validated acquisition category. Israeli startups building adjacent capabilities in AI agent governance, browser-level policy enforcement, or enterprise AI observability are likely next targets and worth sourcing now.

**NVision** | Series B | $55,000,000
[MANDATE FIT: HealthTech]
- What they do: NVision develops quantum-enhanced sensing technology that boosts MRI signals by orders of magnitude, enabling real-time measurement of metabolism on standard MRI systems. The company's POLARIS platform is being deployed at leading cancer centers worldwide. It has also discovered a new type of organic molecule-based qubit and is building photonic integrated quantum circuits (PIQC) for drug discovery.
- Investors: Abbott (lead), European Investment Bank ($17M venture loan). Total raised to date: $120M.
- Why it matters: Abbott leading the round is a strong strategic validation signal. POLARIS systems are scheduled for deployment across 20 centers in the US, Europe, and Asia by year-end 2026. The simultaneous pivot into quantum computing for drug discovery broadens the addressable market significantly.
- Signals: Israeli-founded company operating at the intersection of quantum physics and clinical medicine. The EIB venture loan component suggests strong institutional confidence. Dual-use platform (MRI sensing plus quantum computing) is unusual and expands the company's exit optionality.
- Suggested angle: NVision is past Series A, but the quantum-HealthTech intersection it occupies is generating spinout opportunities. Israeli researchers in adjacent quantum sensing and medical imaging fields may be forming companies worth tracking for earlier-stage investment.

**Frame Security** | Seed | $50,000,000
[MANDATE FIT: Cyber]
- What they do: Frame builds an AI-driven platform for human risk security, using generative AI to create realistic social engineering and deepfake attack simulations and to deliver hyper-personalized, role-based security training. The platform targets the 90% of data breaches that still involve the human element.
- Investors: Index Ventures, Team8, Picture Capital (co-leads). Assaf Rappaport (Wiz CEO) and Elad Gil also participated.
- Why it matters: $50M at seed stage from Index Ventures and Team8 reflects extreme investor conviction. The company already has 20-30 enterprise customers, including AlphaSense and Louis Dreyfus. The global security awareness training market is projected to exceed $10B by 2027.
- Signals: Founders Tal Shlomo (CEO) and Sharon Shmueli (CTO) are Unit 8200 veterans. Shlomo was one of the first employees at Wiz. Shmueli was CTO at Team8's venture platform at 25 and first employee at Bionic (acquired by CrowdStrike). Wiz CEO Rappaport investing personally is a strong signal.
- Suggested angle: Frame is overscaled for a Menomadin check at seed, but the category it is defining, AI-powered human risk security, is nascent. Earlier-stage startups building vertical applications of deepfake detection, voice authentication, or behavioral biometrics for enterprise may emerge in this wave.

**Novella** | Series A | $21,000,000
[MANDATE FIT: Fintech]
- What they do: Novella is an AI-powered wholesale insurance broker targeting the excess and surplus (E&S) market, a $150B+ annual premium segment covering complex risks like skyscrapers in hurricane zones, large infrastructure, and construction sites. AI agents handle placement, binding, policy review, endorsements, and renewals, while human brokers focus on client relationships.
- Investors: Brewer Lane Ventures (lead), Box Group, Crystal Venture Partners, SV Angel, Avid Ventures, Verissimo Ventures, Blank Ventures. Global insurer Arch also participated.
- Why it matters: Novella is licensed in all 50 US states, has been appointed by nearly 100 specialty carriers and MGAs, and has over 3,500 retail agencies sending business to the platform. The combination of regulatory licensing, carrier relationships, and AI-driven automation creates a defensible position in a traditionally manual industry.
- Signals: R&D facility in Tel Aviv with CTO Michael Tsibelman. The company was co-founded in 2024 and has already opened offices in Miami, Houston, and plans southern California in Q2 2026. Arch's participation as both an investor and a carrier partner is a strong commercial validation signal.
- Suggested angle: This is the closest mandate fit this week. Series A stage, $21M round, Fintech sector, Israeli R&D team. The E&S insurance vertical is large and underdigitized. Recommend monitoring Novella's trajectory and sourcing other Israeli startups applying AI agents to complex financial services workflows.

**Base44 / Wix** | Secondary | $38,000,000
[MANDATE FIT: AI]
- What they do: Base44 built a "vibe coding" platform allowing users to create software through natural language prompts rather than traditional coding. Wix acquired the one-person startup for $80M in June 2025. The platform's ARR has since reached $150M, far exceeding original targets.
- Investors: Wix (acquirer). $38M earn-out milestone payment triggered by performance.
- Why it matters: A solo founder building to $150M ARR within 18 months of launch and triggering a $38M earn-out on top of an $80M acquisition price is among the most capital-efficient outcomes in recent Israeli tech history. It demonstrates the viability of the AI-native, zero-employee startup model.
- Signals: Founder Maor Shlomo represents a new archetype: the solo technical founder leveraging AI to build at a pace that previously required teams of 50+. The broader category of AI-powered development tools continues to grow rapidly.
- Suggested angle: Base44 itself is no longer investable, but the "vibe coding" category it validated is producing new entrants. Israeli developers building AI-native dev tools, no-code platforms, or workflow automation tools are worth sourcing. The category is also relevant to portfolio companies evaluating build-vs-buy for internal tooling.

## Section 3: Market Insights

- Cybersecurity remains the dominant deal category. Two of the five deals this week are cyber (LayerX M&A at $205M, Frame Security seed at $50M), and both are specifically targeting AI-era threats. Browser security is now a confirmed acquisition category, and AI-powered social engineering defense is emerging as the next one.

- AI is embedded in every deal this week, but the nature of the AI application is shifting. Rather than horizontal AI infrastructure, this week's deals apply AI to specific verticals: insurance brokerage (Novella), MRI diagnostics (NVision), deepfake defense (Frame), browser security (LayerX), and software development (Base44). This reflects a maturing ecosystem where AI is the enabling layer, not the product category.

- Strategic acquirers are accelerating Israeli M&A. Akamai's LayerX acquisition is its fourth Tel Aviv-based deal in five years. Combined with Cisco's three Israeli acquisitions in three months (reported last week), large platform companies are systematically building AI security stacks from Israeli components. This trend compresses timelines for Israeli cyber startups from founding to exit.

- HealthTech-quantum crossover is a new theme. NVision's $55M Series B, backed by Abbott, sits at the intersection of quantum sensing and clinical medicine. This is the first significant Israeli deal in 2026 that bridges quantum technology and healthcare, a combination worth monitoring as both sectors independently attract capital.

- Deal volume remains below Q1 averages. Five confirmed deals this week, compared to 8-12 per week in Q1 2026. The lighter volume may partly reflect the Mind the Tech conference in New York (May 11-13), where founder attention shifts temporarily from fundraising to networking and business development.

## Section 4: Special Flags

- **Repeat founders (Frame Security):** CEO Tal Shlomo was among the first employees at Wiz (valued at $12B+). CTO Sharon Shmueli was the first employee at Bionic, which CrowdStrike acquired. Both served in Unit 8200. This pedigree, combined with personal investment from Wiz CEO Assaf Rappaport, signals a founding team that tier-1 investors are willing to back at $50M pre-product maturity. Frame's trajectory is worth watching as a competitive reference point for any Menomadin portfolio companies operating in adjacent cyber categories.

- **Competitive threat in Fintech/InsurTech:** Novella's rapid buildout (licensed in all 50 states, 100 carrier appointments, 3,500 agency relationships) with an Israeli R&D backbone represents a model that could be replicated across other complex financial services verticals. Any Menomadin portfolio companies in Fintech should be aware of the AI-agent-powered vertical broker model as a potential competitive dynamic.

- **High-priority opportunity for outreach (Novella):** Novella is the strongest mandate fit this week. Series A stage at $21M, Fintech sector, R&D in Tel Aviv, and applying AI agents to a large, underdigitized vertical. The round size ($16M Series A within $21M total) is slightly above Menomadin's typical check range, but the company's trajectory and sector alignment warrant a direct conversation with the founding team. The E&S insurance market's complexity creates natural defensibility that aligns well with an impact-adjacent investment thesis.

- **M&A signal for portfolio positioning:** The LayerX and Base44 outcomes this week share a common thread: both were acquired relatively early (LayerX had raised $45M total; Base44 was a solo founder) by larger platform companies (Akamai, Wix) seeking AI capabilities. This reinforces that strategic acquirers are paying premium multiples for Israeli startups with demonstrated AI integration. Portfolio companies with strong AI capabilities should be positioned accordingly for potential strategic interest.
