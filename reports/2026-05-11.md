## Section 1: Top Deals

**Cisco / Astrix Security** | M&A | ~$400,000,000
[MANDATE FIT: Cyber]
- What they do: Astrix built a platform securing non-human identities, including API keys, service accounts, and OAuth tokens, that AI agents rely on to access enterprise systems without governance or oversight. Founded in 2021 by Alon Jackson (CEO) and Idan Gour (CTO), both Unit 8200 veterans. Tel Aviv HQ with approximately 85 employees.
- Investors: Previously raised over $85M. Acquired by Cisco for approximately $400M.
- Why it matters: This is Cisco's third Israeli acquisition in under three months, following Armis ($7.75B) and Pyramid Analytics. Cisco is systematically assembling an AI-era security stack, and Astrix fills the critical gap of non-human identity governance. The deal will be integrated into Cisco Identity Intelligence and its zero-trust offerings.
- Signals: The rapid cadence of Cisco's Israeli acquisitions signals that large platform companies view Israeli cyber startups as essential building blocks for AI security infrastructure. Non-human identity management is emerging as a standalone category.
- Suggested angle: The Astrix exit validates demand for AI-agent governance tooling. Early-stage Israeli startups building adjacent capabilities in agent observability, policy enforcement, or identity lifecycle management should be on the radar.

**Kela** | Growth | $200,000,000
- What they do: Kela builds an open operating system for modern militaries, enabling Western armed forces to rapidly integrate commercial technologies into defense systems. The platform aggregates multi-source data, processes it in real time, and presents it through a unified command interface. Founded mid-2024 by Hamutal Meridor, Alon Dror, Jason Manne, and Omer Bar-Ilan.
- Investors: Stripes and D1 Capital Partners (co-leads), Bill Ackman, Eric Schmidt, existing investors Sequoia Capital and Lux Capital.
- Why it matters: Kela reaches unicorn status at a $1.2B valuation less than two years after founding. Contract volume reportedly reached $50M in 2025. The round brings total funding past $300M across multiple rounds completed in rapid succession.
- Signals: Sequoia's continued doubling down on an Israeli defense company is notable. Participation from Ackman and Schmidt signals crossover interest from non-traditional defense investors. The speed from founding to unicorn is among the fastest in Israeli tech history.
- Suggested angle: Kela is well past Series A, but its trajectory validates the thesis that modern defense platforms need commercial-grade software infrastructure. Adjacent opportunities exist in defense data pipelines, interoperability layers, and secure cloud infrastructure for military use cases.

**ZyG** | Series A | $60,000,000
[MANDATE FIT: AI]
- What they do: ZyG automates advertising, retention, customer support, and inventory forecasting for direct-to-consumer e-commerce sellers using AI agents that operate autonomously on platforms like Meta. The company has raised $118M total across seed and Series A in two months. Founded by five ironSource co-founders: Tomer Bar-Zeev (chairman), Omer Kaplan (CEO), Assaf Ben Ami (CFO/COO), Nadav Ashkenazy, and Daniel Shinar.
- Investors: Accel (lead), Felix Capital, Bessemer Venture Partners, Lightspeed Venture Partners.
- Why it matters: $118M raised in approximately two months from seed to Series A at a $500M valuation. Accel leading the Series A after Bessemer and Lightspeed seeded the company signals strong competitive positioning in agentic commerce.
- Signals: Repeat founders from ironSource ($4.4B merger with Unity in 2022). Team also includes cybersecurity and AI specialists from Unit 81. This is a pure founder-quality bet at speed.
- Suggested angle: ZyG is too far ahead on valuation for a Series A fund. However, the agentic e-commerce category it is defining is worth mapping for earlier-stage entrants building vertical AI agents for commerce workflows.

---

**CopilotKit** | Series A | $27,000,000
[MANDATE FIT: AI]
- What they do: CopilotKit builds the interface layer between humans and AI agents inside software applications. Its open-source AG-UI protocol defines how AI agents interact with users in real time, enabling dynamic interfaces with streaming responses, live UI updates, and cross-interaction context. Founded by Israeli brothers Atai Barkai (CEO, formerly Meta) and Uli Barkai.
- Investors: Glilot Capital, NFX, SignalFire (co-leads).
- Why it matters: AG-UI has been adopted by Google, Microsoft, Amazon, and Oracle. CopilotKit claims more than half of the Fortune 500 use its tools. Open-source adoption at this pace is unusual and suggests strong product-market fit in AI agent infrastructure.
- Signals: Israeli-founded, US-headquartered with a 25-person development center in Israel. The enterprise adoption curve for an open-source protocol serving the agent stack is steep and accelerating.
- Suggested angle: CopilotKit is at Series A and within the mandate check size range. The company sits at the intersection of AI infrastructure and developer tooling with blue-chip enterprise adoption already in place. Recommend initiating a direct conversation with the founders.

## Section 3: Market Insights

- Defense tech continues to command outsized rounds. Kela's $200M raise at a $1.2B valuation, combined with D-Fend Solutions exploring a $1B exit (see Section 4), confirms that defense technology remains the highest-momentum category in the Israeli ecosystem. Post-October 7 capital flows into defense show no sign of slowing.

- Cisco's Israeli acquisition spree is reshaping cyber M&A. Three acquisitions in under three months (Armis at $7.75B, Pyramid Analytics, and now Astrix at ~$400M) represents a systematic strategy to build an AI-era security platform from Israeli components. Other large platform companies are likely to follow.

- AI agent infrastructure is the dominant theme. Three of four deals this week involve AI agents directly: ZyG (agentic commerce), CopilotKit (agent-human interface protocol), and Astrix (agent identity security). The Israeli ecosystem is moving decisively from "AI models" to "AI agents in production."

- Repeat founders continue to attract premium valuations and compressed timelines. ZyG's ironSource founding team raised $118M in approximately two months at a $500M valuation. This reinforces the pattern that Israeli repeat founders with successful exits command outsized rounds with minimal friction.

- Relatively light deal volume this week. Four confirmed deals above $500K, compared to typical weekly averages of 8-12 during Q1 2026. This may reflect seasonal factors rather than a structural shift, given that Israeli startups raised over $1.25B in April alone.

## Section 4: Special Flags

- **Repeat founders (ZyG):** Five co-founders previously built ironSource, which merged with Unity for $4.4B in 2022. Tomer Bar-Zeev and Omer Kaplan are among the most experienced founding teams currently operating in the Israeli startup landscape. Their ability to raise $118M pre-product-maturity is a direct function of track record.

- **Potential high-value exit (D-Fend Solutions):** Israeli counter-drone company D-Fend has hired Goldman Sachs to explore a sale at approximately $1B valuation, reported around May 7. The company's EnforceAir system is deployed across 30 countries, including US, Canadian, and Australian defense and security agencies. Its last round in early 2024 valued it at ~$250M, making this a potential 4x step-up. Not yet a confirmed transaction.

- **High-priority opportunity for outreach (CopilotKit):** CopilotKit is the strongest mandate fit this week. Series A stage, $27M raised, building AI agent infrastructure with proven Fortune 500 adoption (Google, Microsoft, Amazon, Oracle). Israeli founders with development center in Tel Aviv. Round size and stage align with Menomadin's check size and investment thesis. Recommend outreach this week.

- **Competitive signal in cyber M&A:** Cisco's pattern of three Israeli acquisitions in three months creates secondary dynamics. Startups building in adjacent spaces (AI governance, non-human identity, agent security) may see increased inbound interest from other strategic acquirers seeking to match Cisco's positioning. This raises both opportunity and competitive threat for mandate-sector investments in Cyber.
