### SECTION 1: TOP DEALS

**Credo / DustPhotonics** | M&A | $750M–$1.3B
[MANDATE FIT: AI]
- What they do: DustPhotonics develops co-packaged optics for AI data center interconnects; acquired by Credo Technology to strengthen its high-speed connectivity portfolio.
- Investors: Credo Technology (acquirer); prior backers include Intel Capital and Samsung Ventures.
- Why it matters: Validates the AI infrastructure buildout thesis — optical interconnect is a chokepoint in scaling large clusters, and strategic acquirers are paying premium multiples to solve it.
- Signals: Exit multiple estimated at 8–10x revenue; deal signals consolidation in the AI hardware stack layer.
- Suggested angle: Monitor adjacent Israeli deep-tech plays in silicon photonics and co-packaged optics at Series A stage.

---

**Linx Security** | Series B | $50M
[MANDATE FIT: Cyber]
- What they do: Identity security platform focused on non-human identities (service accounts, API keys, machine credentials) — an underserved attack surface as enterprises move to cloud-native architectures.
- Investors: Insight Partners (lead); participation from Bessemer Venture Partners.
- Why it matters: Non-human identity is emerging as the fastest-growing threat vector in enterprise security; Linx is early in a category that will attract significant M&A interest from CrowdStrike, SentinelOne, and Palo Alto.
- Signals: Tier-1 lead investor; category-defining product in a nascent but fast-moving space.
- Suggested angle: Track for Series C momentum; could be a benchmark for pricing identity security rounds.

---

**AI21 Labs / Nebius** | M&A (in talks) | Multi-billion
[MANDATE FIT: AI]
- What they do: AI21 Labs builds enterprise LLM infrastructure and applied AI tools; Nebius is a cloud AI platform spun out of Yandex. Talks focus on a strategic merger or acquisition.
- Why it matters: If completed, creates a significant Israeli-origin AI infrastructure entity with European cloud distribution — strategic rather than financial exit.
- Signals: Both companies have been repositioning post-ChatGPT disruption; deal would be one of the largest Israeli tech transactions of 2026.
- Suggested angle: Watch deal structure — could influence how Series A AI infrastructure companies position for exits.

---

**Q-Factor** | Seed | $24M
- What they do: Quantum error correction software for near-term quantum hardware; technology spun out of the Weizmann Institute.
- Investors: Grove Ventures (lead); Pitango.
- Why it matters: One of the largest seed rounds in Israeli deep tech this quarter; indicates investor appetite for long-cycle bets in quantum.
- Signals: Academic spin-out with strong IP moat; long commercialization timeline.
- Suggested angle: Outside Menomadin mandate but worth tracking as a signal of deep-tech risk appetite.

---

**Haat** | Growth | $20M
[MANDATE FIT: AgriTech]
- What they do: B2B marketplace and logistics platform connecting Israeli agricultural producers to export markets across Europe and the Gulf.
- Investors: OurCrowd (lead); participation from an undisclosed Gulf-based family office.
- Why it matters: Gulf normalization is creating real commercial traction in Israeli agri-export; Haat is positioned as the infrastructure layer for that corridor.
- Signals: Gulf investor participation signals regional demand validation; OurCrowd lead is a positive signal for platform businesses with network effects.
- Suggested angle: Adjacent to Menomadin's AgriTech mandate — worth an introductory call to understand unit economics and expansion roadmap.

### SECTION 3: MARKET INSIGHTS

- **M&A dominated the week.** Two major transactions (DustPhotonics exit, AI21/Nebius talks) overshadowed the new funding activity, suggesting the larger Israeli tech market is entering a consolidation phase after two years of valuation reset.
- **Cybersecurity and AI remain the most active sectors.** Linx Security (identity governance) and the AI21 situation both reinforce that enterprise security and AI infrastructure are attracting the most institutional capital.
- **Gulf-to-Israel deal flow is accelerating.** The Haat round included Gulf-based LP participation, continuing a pattern observed in Q1 2026 where Gulf family offices are increasingly direct participants in Israeli tech rounds — not just through intermediaries.
- **Lighter new-round volume than recent weeks.** The week's new primary funding rounds were fewer than usual, likely a pre-conference pause ahead of Israel Tech Week Miami (April 27–30).
- **Grove Ventures appeared twice** (Q-Factor seed, LP activity) — one of the more active Israeli early-stage VCs this week.

- **Opportunity — Haat (AgriTech):** Direct overlap with Menomadin's AgriTech mandate. The Gulf investor angle and OurCrowd lead are positive signals. Recommend an introductory call before Israel Tech Week Miami to assess whether a follow-on or co-investment opportunity exists.
