## Section 1: Top Deals

**VAST Data** | Series F | ~$1,000,000,000 (at $30B valuation)
[MANDATE FIT: AI]
- What they do: Israeli-founded AI data infrastructure platform providing unified storage, database, and data pipeline capabilities purpose-built for training and deploying AI models at scale.
- Investors: Drive Capital (lead), Access Industries (co-lead), with NVIDIA, Fidelity Management & Research, and NEA.
- Why it matters: This is the largest single funding event in Israeli tech in 2026 so far. A 3x valuation jump from $9.1B to $30B in under three years signals that enterprise AI infrastructure has entered a new pricing tier entirely.
- Signals: Co-founder Renen Hallak is a Technion graduate and former VP R&D at XtremIO (acquired by Dell EMC). ~370 of the company's 1,100 employees are based in Israel. NVIDIA's continued participation signals deep strategic alignment.
- Suggested angle: VAST Data is a category-defining platform company, not a direct fit for Series A investment, but its ecosystem of Israeli-based suppliers and technical talent creates downstream opportunities. Monitor early-stage Israeli startups building tooling or vertical applications on top of VAST's data fabric.

**CathWorks (acquired by Medtronic)** | M&A | $585,000,000
[MANDATE FIT: HealthTech]
- What they do: Developer of FFRangio, an AI-powered diagnostic system that assesses the entire coronary artery tree from standard angiograms, replacing the need for invasive pressure-wire procedures.
- Investors: Acquirer is Medtronic. CathWorks previously raised from Corning Ventures, GE Healthcare, and others.
- Why it matters: This acquisition, which closed on April 20, validates the thesis that AI-driven diagnostics in cardiology can reach the scale required for medtech majors to pay premium prices. The deal includes additional undisclosed earn-out payments beyond the $585M base.
- Signals: Medtronic and CathWorks had a commercial partnership since 2022 for FFRangio distribution in the US, Europe, and Japan. The acquisition converts a proven distribution relationship into full ownership, a pattern worth tracking across other Israeli healthtech partnerships.
- Suggested angle: This exit reinforces the opportunity in AI-augmented medical diagnostics. Israeli healthtech startups building non-invasive or AI-native imaging tools for other specialties (oncology, neurology, orthopedics) may see increased investor and acquirer interest.

**ActionAI** | Seed | $10,000,000
[MANDATE FIT: AI]
- What they do: Builds infrastructure to improve the reliability and trustworthiness of AI in enterprise settings. The platform maps data across the AI stack for granular evaluation, testing, and real-time debugging of AI outputs.
- Investors: Led by UAE-based investors (undisclosed).
- Why it matters: As AI moves into regulated, mission-critical workflows (finance, insurance, legal, supply chain), the reliability layer becomes essential. ActionAI targets the gap between prototype AI and production-grade AI in industries where errors carry material consequences.
- Signals: Founded by Miriam Haart, a Stanford-trained engineer. The company is dual-headquartered in New York and Israel. UAE-based lead investment reflects growing Gulf capital interest in Israeli AI infrastructure.
- Suggested angle: AI reliability and observability is becoming a crowded space globally, but ActionAI's focus on regulated industries and Middle Eastern investor backing creates a differentiated positioning. Worth a direct conversation to assess product depth and competitive moat.

**Copperhelm** | Seed | $7,000,000
[MANDATE FIT: Cyber]
- What they do: Agentic cloud security platform where AI agents autonomously monitor cloud environments, investigate threats, and execute remediation in real time, built around a proprietary Context Lake that structures and connects cloud data across environments.
- Investors: TLV Partners (lead), ICON, toDay Ventures, SaaS Ventures Israel.
- Why it matters: Copperhelm emerged from stealth on April 23 with paying Fortune 500 customers already on the platform. This is the latest signal that agentic security, where AI replaces manual SOC workflows, is moving from concept to production faster than expected.
- Signals: Founded by Shimon Tolts (ex-Unity), Eyar Zilberman (ex-McAfee), and Roman Labunsky (ex-RSA). The founding team combines cloud infrastructure experience with deep cybersecurity credentials, a combination increasingly valued by enterprise buyers.
- Suggested angle: At $7M Seed with Fortune 500 customers, Copperhelm is at the stage where a Series A fund should be building a relationship. The agentic security category is heating up fast (Cylake at $45M Seed, Above Security at $43M Series A in recent months), and early movers with enterprise traction will command premium terms at Series A.

## Section 3: Market Insights

- **Independence Day week dampened deal volume.** April 22 was Israel's 78th Independence Day (Yom Ha'atzmaut), and the traditional quiet around the holiday reduced the number of new deal announcements. The week produced only four confirmed deals, though the VAST Data and CathWorks closings pushed aggregate capital well above $1.6 billion.

- **AI infrastructure is commanding mega-round pricing.** VAST Data's $30B valuation, combined with the broader Q1 trend of 40%+ of Israeli capital flowing into early rounds, suggests that AI-native infrastructure companies are absorbing a disproportionate share of available venture capital. This dynamic could crowd out mid-stage companies that need growth capital.

- **Agentic security is the dominant cyber sub-theme.** In the past two months alone, Cylake ($45M Seed), Above Security ($43M Series A), Copperhelm ($7M Seed), Onyx Security ($40M), and Bold ($28M Series A) have all raised for platforms that deploy autonomous AI agents for security tasks. Israeli cyber's next wave is no longer about detection; it is about autonomous response.

- **HealthTech exits are validating the sector.** The CathWorks acquisition at $585M follows a broader pattern of medtech acquirers converting Israeli AI diagnostics partnerships into full acquisitions. For a fund like Menomadin with HealthTech as a core mandate sector, this creates a clearer path-to-exit narrative for early-stage portfolio companies.

- **Israel Tech Week Miami begins April 27.** The event draws 1,500+ founders, VCs, and decision-makers across nine technology verticals. Several deal announcements may have been timed for the event, and the coming week could see a spike in disclosed rounds.

- **Gulf capital is becoming a recurring theme.** ActionAI's UAE-led Seed round adds to a growing pattern of Gulf sovereign and institutional capital flowing into Israeli startups, diversifying the investor base beyond traditional US and European VC.

The rapid succession of large rounds (Cylake $45M, Above Security $43M, Copperhelm $7M, Bold $28M, Onyx $40M) indicates that the agentic security category is approaching a tipping point. Any Menomadin portfolio company in adjacent cyber spaces should be aware of the competitive intensity.

**High-priority opportunities worth reaching out on this week:**
- **Copperhelm** is the strongest Series A pipeline candidate from this week. At $7M Seed with Fortune 500 customers, the company sits squarely in Menomadin's check-size range for a follow-on or co-investment at Series A. The agentic cloud security thesis is gaining momentum rapidly, and the TLV Partners-led Seed suggests a path to a premium Series A in 6-12 months. Recommend initiating a relationship now.
