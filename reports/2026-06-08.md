## Section 1: Top Deals

**DriveNets** | Series D | $410M
[MANDATE FIT: AI]
- What they do: Develops software-based networking solutions for AI data centers and telecom operators, replacing proprietary hardware with disaggregated, cloud-native network infrastructure.
- Investors: Bessemer Venture Partners and Atreides Management (co-leads); AMD, Red Dot Capital, Pitango, D1 Capital Partners
- Why it matters: The $8.5B valuation and AMD's strategic entry signal that AI networking infrastructure is becoming as contested as compute. DriveNets is cash-flow positive with over $1B in secured business, a rarity at this stage.
- Signals: Already cash-flow positive since 2025. AMD joining as a strategic investor validates the hardware-software convergence thesis. Total raised now exceeds $1B.
- Suggested angle: DriveNets is a category leader, but the AI networking stack it sits in is spawning adjacent startups at Series A. Track companies building complementary tooling for AI fabric management and network orchestration.

**Cyera** | Series G | $300M
[MANDATE FIT: Cyber]

- Investors: Evolution Equity Partners (lead); Georgian, Greenoaks, Lightspeed, Sequoia, Sapphire Ventures, Redpoint, Cyberstarts, Coatue, Accel, Spark Capital
- Why it matters: Valuation doubled from $6B to $12B in five months. Cyera is now the second-most valuable private Israeli company after Vast Data ($30B). The investor syndicate reads like a who's who of global venture capital.
- Signals: Talpiot and Unit 8200 founders. Total raised exceeds $2B. The pace of valuation growth suggests either a pre-IPO trajectory or a significant strategic exit.
- Suggested angle: The data security category Cyera dominates will generate deal flow at the Series A layer as enterprises look for complementary point solutions. Monitor startups addressing data governance for AI training pipelines.

**ZutaCore** | Series C | $100M
[MANDATE FIT: Energy]
- What they do: Develops waterless two-phase direct-to-chip liquid cooling for AI processors and high-performance computing, cutting energy use in half while eliminating water consumption.
- Investors: Mitsubishi Electric, Carrier Ventures, Samsung Ventures (Samsung Electronics CVC arm)
- Why it matters: Three major industrial strategics co-investing signals validation from companies that will be deploying this technology at scale. ZutaCore's approach addresses next-gen chips exceeding 4,000 watts, a thermal challenge the industry has not yet solved.
- Signals: 75+ deployments worldwide. Valuation at ~$600M with ~$200M total raised. Strategic investor composition (Mitsubishi, Carrier, Samsung) is unusual for a VC round and suggests near-term commercial pull rather than speculative positioning.
- Suggested angle: Strong impact-mandate fit given the energy and water dimensions. ZutaCore is likely past Menomadin's check-size range, but the data center cooling ecosystem includes earlier-stage Israeli companies worth screening.

## Section 3: Market Insights

- **AI infrastructure dominance continues.** Five of the eight deals this week are directly tied to the AI stack: networking (DriveNets), observability (Coralogix), cooling (ZutaCore), agent governance (Willow), and data security for AI environments (Cyera). Capital continues to flow toward the infrastructure layer that enables AI rather than application-layer plays.

- **Cybersecurity remains Israel's signature category.** Cyera's $12B valuation makes it the second-most valuable private Israeli company. Combined with Willow's seed round in AI agent governance, the data suggests the security conversation is shifting from protecting human users to governing autonomous AI agents.

- **The K-shaped market is sharpening.** The week's $1.07B total was dominated by three mega-rounds (DriveNets, Cyera, Coralogix) accounting for 85% of capital. The gap between category leaders raising at $1B+ valuations and seed-stage companies is widening, leaving a thin middle market for Series A/B companies.

- **Strategic corporates are writing checks.** AMD (DriveNets), Mitsubishi/Carrier/Samsung (ZutaCore), and Zendesk (Rep AI) all participated as strategic investors this week. This pattern suggests that corporates are increasingly using venture as a procurement pipeline, not just a financial instrument.

- **Layoffs and fundraising are running in parallel.** CTech reported that this $1.1B week coincided with major layoffs: Wix cutting 1,000 jobs (20% of workforce) and Skai laying off ~100 employees. The industry is simultaneously attracting record capital and shedding human headcount, a structural dynamic driven by AI-led productivity gains.

## Section 4: Special Flags

- **Repeat founder signal at Cyera.** CEO Yotam Segev and CTO Tamar Bar-Ilan are both Talpiot graduates and Unit 8200 veterans. While Cyera is their first major venture, the Talpiot pedigree and the speed of scaling ($2B+ raised, $12B valuation in under five years) make this founding team one to track across future ventures.

- **Competitive threat to mandate sectors.** Cyera's expanding data security platform may encroach on the AI governance space where Willow is positioning. As Cyera builds out agent-level security capabilities, it could compress the window for standalone AI governance startups. Menomadin should assess Willow's defensibility against this competitive vector before engaging.

- **High-priority opportunity: Willow (Cyber/AI Agent Governance).** $7M Seed led by Hetz Ventures. AI agent governance sits at the intersection of Menomadin's cyber mandate and the fastest-growing enterprise compliance need. Already deployed at Wix with real usage data. Early enough for a Series A entry within 12-18 months. Recommend proactive outreach this week.

- **High-priority opportunity: Centrical (B2B SaaS/Workforce AI).** $39M Series D after a four-year fundraising gap. The company's pivot to AI-era workforce optimization is generating renewed traction at enterprise scale. While the Series D is above Menomadin's typical entry point, Centrical's repositioning validates the AI-workforce optimization space as ripe for new Series A entrants.

- **Hetz Ventures' new $140M fund (Fund IV)** is deploying into cyber and AI infrastructure seed deals. As both an LP signal and a co-investor, Hetz's portfolio will likely surface multiple mandate-fit opportunities over the next 12 months. Maintain the relationship.
